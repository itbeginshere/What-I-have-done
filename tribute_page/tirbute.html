Alan Watts

“The meaning of life is just to be alive. It is so plain and so obvious and so simple. And yet, everybody rushes around in a great panic as if it were necessary to achieve something beyond themselves.”

A brief summary of Alan Watts:

  • 1915: Born in Chislehurst village in Kent, United Kingdom
  • 1932: Produced his first booklet, an Outline of Zen Buddhism
  • 1936: Attended the World Congress of Faiths at the Univserity of London
  • 1936: Published his first book, "The Spirit of Zen"
  • 1938: Moved to United States to study Zen in New York
  • 1940: Published "The Meaning of Happiness" a book based on his talks
  • 1944: Ordained as an Episcopal priest
  • 1950: Resigned from church as a preist
  • 1951: Moved to San Francisco to teach Buddhist studies
  • 1953: Published "Myth & Ritual in Christianity"
  • 1953: Started a radio show, "The Great Books of Asia"
  • 1956: Started a radio show, "Way Beyond the West"
  • 1957: Published "The Way of Zen", his most well-known books
  • 1960: Became increasingly interested in how identifiable patterns in nature tend to repeat themselves.
  • 1966: Published "The Book: On the Taboo Against Knowing Who You Are"
  • 1973: Passes away at the age of 58 in his sleep
